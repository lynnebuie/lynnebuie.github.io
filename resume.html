Geospatial Experience

GIS Analyst

The Scottish Government

September 2015 - May 2017

Handled ad-hoc GIS requests from all of central government, ranging in scale from basic maps with quick turn around to long scale projects with considerable work undertaken to define customer needs. Frequently managed several projects simultaneously, managing deadlines and keeping customers informed.

Worked on three national spatial indicators: a measure of deprivation in scottish society, access to greenspace in scottish cities and nationwide availability of public transportation. The latter involved lengthy discussions which I led with the customer to define purpose and scope, which I used to determine an approriate analysis methodology and delivery schedule.

Utilised Python programming for various tasks and projects, including management of large quantities of files and utilising Python to streamline complex analyses. For example, I developed a tool to quantify population and area differences between administrative boundaries, and integrated this into a custom ArcGIS Toolbox.

Wrote and published metadata and OGC web services using ArcGIS for Server. Published these on the outwardly facing Scottish Spatial Data Infrastructure site. Involved with testing during the devlopment of this site, and writing of the online documentation.

Presented results of a year long analysis project at a the annual scottish public sector One Scotland Mapping Agreement conference in front of several hundred delegates. Presented twice on GIS in the Scottish Government at the MSc GIS career fair at the University of Edinburgh.

The University of Edinburgh

Supported students during computer laboratories in the subjects of GIS (Quantum GIS) and climate modelling (Microsoft Excel).

Provided teaching and logistical support for a final year Physical Geography residential field course in Iceland.

Research Intern

The Centre for Ecology and Hydrology

June 2014 - August 2014

Conducted a self-led research project investigating carbon dioxide flux in a peatland. This involved regular field data collection, data analysis using R, acquisition and analysis of satellite data, and presentation of results to colleagues in the research centre.
